Guidelines for the Final Report

1.  Clearly describe the experimental setup
    For every part of the experimental setup, including hardware, software, and workloads, clearly distinguish between:

        the original setup described in the paper, and
        the setup you actually used.

Also discuss how any differences between the two setups may have affected your results. 2. Document the effort required to build the setup
Include a brief discussion of the difficulty of establishing the experimental environment. For example:

    How much (unexpected) effort was needed?
    What problems or obstacles arose?
    What changes or fixes were necessary before experiments could run?

3.  Separate reproduction from stress-testing
    Organize the report so that it clearly distinguishes between:

        attempts to reproduce the paper’s original results, and
        attempts to “break” or stress-test the index.

These are different goals and should be discussed in separate sections. 4. Present reproduction results systematically
For the reproduction portion, address the following:

    Were you able to observe similar overall trends to those reported in the paper?
    Which results were consistent with the original paper?
    Which results were different?
    For the differences, what explains them?
    For results you were unable to reproduce, explain why.

5.  Present “break” or stress-test results systematically
    For the stress-testing portion, address the following:

        How did you attempt to break or expose weaknesses in the index?
        In what way did the index fail, degrade, or behave unexpectedly?
        Why do you think those failures or weaknesses occurred?

6.  Improve result presentation for direct comparison
    When presenting graphs and figures:

        Use the same units and axis scales as the original paper whenever possible.
        Place your results side by side with the paper’s results to make comparison easy.
        Aim to make similarities and differences immediately visible to the reader.
